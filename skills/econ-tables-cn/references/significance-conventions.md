# Significance Conventions

Use significance stars only as a compact reporting convention, never as a substitute for effect-size interpretation.
Baseline convention for economics, finance, and management tables:
- * means p < 0.10.
- ** means p < 0.05.
- *** means p < 0.01.
- Report exact p-values where the target journal prefers them.
- Always define the convention in a table note.
- Keep one convention across manuscript, appendix, and slides.
- Do not mix t statistics and standard errors without labeling.
- For small samples, discuss inference sensitivity rather than relying on stars.
- For multiple testing, report correction or false-discovery control if relevant.
- For IV tables, report first-stage relevance and weak instrument diagnostics.
- For DiD tables, stars do not replace pre-trend and design evidence.
- For nonlinear models, clarify whether coefficients, marginal effects, or odds ratios are shown.
- For survey studies, pair stars with reliability and validity evidence.
- For Management Science and Journal of Finance, use clean notes and avoid visual clutter.
- For 经济研究 and 管理世界, Chinese table notes should define stars and standard errors clearly.
